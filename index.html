Chimeras: Human beings with multiple sets of DNA

Imagine giving birth to a child that does not match your DNA sequence

So what is a chimera?

The name comes from Greek mythology. The Chimera was a fearsome, fire-breathing creature with a lion's head, a goat's body, and a dragon's tail, essentially a being amalgamated from different species. From a scientific standpoint, a chimera is an organism whose cells are derived from two or more zygotes. During the embryonic phase, the cell cumuli that would eventually form two different beings could fuse forming a single zigot containing the DNA genome of both offspring. Eventually, a new organism would form containing tissues from both zigots.

How were chimeras discovered?

The first was a case of a woman, Karen, who was told, following DNA tests of family members for a kidney donor, that two of her three sons could not be hers. Similar cases were later reported where the main tissues in the individuals' bodies possessed an "X" DNA sequence, while the reproductive organs were made up of "Y" DNA strands.

And are there any risks?

When speaking about artifical chimeras, scientists are also able to create such individuals by combining the DNA of different species. Recently, a pig-human chimera was created to run medical tests with a higher accuracy in terms of results. As it would be expected, these research trials could eventually lead to the creation of individuals with merged human and animal DNA. Can you imagine what animals with human brain tissue would be capable of?
